Mukbang 먹방

Mukbang, muk-bang or meokbang is a live online audiovisual broadcast in which a host eats food while interacting with their audience.

Hibiscus syriacus

Known as the Korean rose, is the national flower of South Korea. The flower appears in national emblems, and Korea is compared poetically to the flower in the South Korean national anthem.The flower's name in Korean is mugunghwa (목근화/무궁화 (Hangul) or 木槿花/無窮花 (Hanja)).

Dak Galbi

Dak-galbi, or spicy stir-fried chicken, is a popular Korean dish made by stir-frying marinated diced chicken in a gochujang-based sauce with sweet potatoes, cabbage, perilla leaves, scallions, tteok, and other ingredients.

Hanbok

A traditional Korean dress for semi-formal or formal attire during traditional occasions such as festivals, celebrations, and ceremonies. It is characterized by vibrant colors and simple lines without pockets.

Kimchi

South Korea’s national dish is kimchi, which is a combination of vegetables and spices that have been fermented underground for months. It is served with almost everything, and eaten at nearly every meal.

Hwarang

Also known as Flowering Knights, were an elite warrior group of male youth in Silla, an ancient kingdom of the Korean Peninsula that lasted until the 10th century. Originally, the hwarang were known for their use of make-up and cosmetic decorations and accessories.
